Incoterms

Commonly Applied Incoterms

International trade transactions conducted by the company follow Incoterms rules issued by the International Chamber of Commerce to define transportation, risk, insurance, and customs responsibilities.

EXW - Ex Works

The seller makes the goods available at their premises. The buyer assumes responsibility for transportation, export clearance, and international shipment.

FOB - Free On Board

The seller delivers the goods on board the vessel at the designated port of shipment. Risk transfers once the cargo is loaded.

CIF - Cost, Insurance and Freight

The seller arranges transportation and minimum insurance coverage to the destination port while risk transfers after loading.

CFR - Cost and Freight

The seller pays the transportation cost to the destination port, while the buyer assumes risk after loading at origin.

DAP - Delivered at Place

The seller is responsible for delivery to the agreed destination. The buyer handles import clearance and duties.

DDP - Delivered Duty Paid

The seller assumes responsibility for transportation, customs clearance, duties, and delivery to the buyer's specified location.
